Sprint 5 - Core Blog

Reflecting on Foundations:

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

1. The impact of the learning environment and the distractions around me, and tools I can use to create an optimal learning environment for myself.
2. The importance of adopting the 'growth mindset' and embracing challenges as a method to learn more effectively.
3. The role of values in my motivation, and how aligning my career choices with my values means a more fulfilling experience.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Understanding my values will help me make more informed decisions about my career path, and ensure that I am working on projects that align with my goals and values. Empathy and self-awareness are important tools to work effectively with others. They help to understand others' perspectives and motivations, which is a good starting point for aligning incentives and setting common goals to work out as a group.


What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I really enjoyed the learnings about neuroplasticity - and the extensive amount of material available to research this subject. Its a really important topic to internalise


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Writing the blogs to succintly sum up the learnings is quite difficult. Even if you think you learned something valuable getting it out into words which form a sensible narrative and convey information & understanding is another thing altogether.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

As the tutors have said, this has been the feedback from students and employers about what skills make better employees.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think that this time could be better spent practising programming, because we already have quite a full schedule of learning and feels like there is a limit to how much you can do. I think the 'research' sections of the core learning was good and valuable, but I felt like the 'asssement' parts (writing blogs etc.) was tedious.
